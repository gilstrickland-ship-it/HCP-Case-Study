# Case Study Brief (as received)

**Role context:** PM (product manager), Housecall Pro — FinTech Mobile Team. The team helps Pros
capture every dollar and get paid faster and on time through various payment
methods. Yet despite all the various payment methods, millions of dollars in
unpaid invoices persist.

**Assignment:** Identify the highest-leverage opportunity to improve getting Pros
paid and on time. Design a solution that uses AI as a meaningful lever and build a
working prototype. AI is expected to be central; a non-AI solution requires an
exceptional, well-grounded case.

**The prototype and artifacts must address:**
1. **Problem framing** — specific pain point and why; what "good" and failure look like.
2. **Solution design** — approach and rationale; what AI does autonomously vs.
   hands back to a human; one viable non-AI alternative considered and why AI wins.
3. **Trust and failure** — how the customer interacts when the system is wrong or
   uncertain; how trust builds over time.
4. **Measurement** — outcome metrics; AI quality bar before shipping.

**Deliverables (submitted ≥24h before a 1-hour session):**
- Working prototype — functional, demoable, built with AI coding tools; can be
  lightweight/partially mocked if core interaction, failure handling, and eval
  plan are demoable.
- Process folder — problem framing doc, research/competitive references,
  PRD (product requirements document), evidence of LLM (large language model)
  iteration before building.
- Eval spec (1 page max) — success criteria, error taxonomy, mini test set of
  10–20 examples, runnable by a teammate next week.
- AI usage log — tools used and for what; top 2–3 prompts/workflows; ≥1 place AI
  was wrong and what was done; ≥1 thing deliberately done without AI and why.

**Session structure:** 10 min process walkthrough → 15 min demo → 30 min live
iteration (panel acts as customers; iterate with AI tools in real time) → 5 min
debrief.

**Guidance:** ~3–5 hours of active work; judgment about what to build is what's
evaluated, not polish. Jumping straight to building without planning is a red
flag. Must be reproducible/extensible live.
