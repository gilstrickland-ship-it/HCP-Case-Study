# AR (Accounts Receivable) & Late-Payment Statistics

Industry context for the scale and cost of unpaid/late invoices, with emphasis on
home services and SMBs (small and mid-sized businesses). AR — accounts receivable —
is the money a business is owed for work already invoiced but not yet paid. All
figures from public sources, gathered 2026-07-06.

## Scale of the problem

- **56% of US small businesses** report money owed from unpaid invoices, averaging
  **$17.5K per business**. 47% have invoices overdue by 30+ days; nearly 1 in 10
  invoices falls into that bucket on average.
  ([QuickBooks 2025 US Small Business Late Payments Report](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/r/small-business-data/small-business-late-payments-report-2025/))
- **More than 2/3 of construction contractors are paid late** (2025 Built
  Technologies study), driving higher costs, inflated bids, and project delays.
  ([Old National Bank summary](https://www.oldnational.com/resources/insights/unpaid-customer-invoices-are-piling-up-squeezing-small-businesses/))
- Roughly **a third of independent-contractor invoices are late** (Bonsai study of
  100,000 contractors). ([Clockify late-invoice statistics](https://clockify.me/late-invoice-statistics))
- **73% of companies** report customer delinquency increased over the past year;
  late payments cost an average of **~$40K/year** per business (PYMNTS 2025 puts
  it at $39,406). ([Kaplan Group B2B payment-delay statistics](https://www.kaplancollectionagency.com/business-advice/54-statistics-on-the-b2b-payment-delays/), [FundTap](https://fundtap.co/resources/why-chasing-invoices-costs-more-than-you-think))

## Time cost of chasing payment

- **87% of US businesses** deal with late payments; small firms spend an average of
  **14 hours per week** on admin related to collecting payments — ~700 hours/year.
  ([FundTap](https://fundtap.co/resources/why-chasing-invoices-costs-more-than-you-think), [Chaser — time cost of late payments](https://www.chaserhq.com/blog/the-time-cost-of-late-payments))
- That time goes to: reminder emails, phone calls, waiting on responses,
  escalation, and following up on broken payment promises.

## DSO benchmarks (home services trades)

DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) = the average number of days between invoicing a
customer and actually receiving the money. Lower is better.

([ClearReceivables DSO by industry](https://clearreceivables.com/blog/days-sales-outstanding-by-industry), [CreditPulse 2025 benchmarks](https://www.creditpulse.com/blog/days-sales-outstanding-dso-by-industry-2025-benchmarks-data-analysis))

| Trade | Typical DSO | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Landscaping / grounds | 25–40 days | Recurring contracts pay reliably; project work runs longer |
| Plumbing | 30–50 days | Emergency calls often paid on the spot; scheduled/commercial work drags |
| Electrical | 40–60 days | Commercial pushes DSO up; residential should target <30 |
| Roofing | 25–45 days | Insurance restoration extends to 45–60; direct-to-customer should collect within 14 |

General benchmark: 30–45 days DSO is considered healthy for services.

## Why customers pay late (root causes)

([Atradius via Clockify](https://clockify.me/late-invoice-statistics), [Propeller Aero on construction](https://www.propelleraero.com/blog/why-are-late-payments-the-norm-in-construction-reasons-and-solutions/))

1. **Customer cash-flow pressure** — the top driver of overdue B2B
   (business-to-business) invoices in the US (~43%).
2. **Process friction** — forgotten invoices, missed due dates, inability to pay
   online easily.
3. **Invoice disputes** — disagreements over charges, quantities, or terms play an
   outsized role in delays.
4. **Intentional float** (more common in B2B/construction chains) — payers hold
   funds to manage their own risk and cash position.

Implication worth noting: late payment is not one behavior — it splits into
*forgot*, *can't pay right now*, *disputes the bill*, and *won't pay*. Each has a
different remedy and a different tone requirement.

## AI in accounts receivable (adoption & results)

([Billtrust — State of AI in AR 2025](https://www.billtrust.com/resources/blog/the-state-of-ai-in-accounts-receivable-2025), [Billtrust DSO study](https://www.billtrust.com/news/study-finds-ai-in-accounts-receivable-reduces-dso))

- ~67% of US SMBs use some automated AR processing; 55% of US small businesses
  used AI by 2025 (68% among 10–100-employee companies).
- **99% of companies using AI in AR reduced average DSO**; 75% report a reduction
  of 6+ days. Automated-AR firms average 40 days DSO vs. 47 for non-automated.
- Trust posture of finance leaders: **40% "AI pragmatists"** (AI assists within
  human-controlled limits), **26% skeptics** (narrow tasks only), **29% advocates**
  (AI runs with guardrails). Autonomy defaults matter.
- 71% plan to increase AI-in-AR investment in the next year.
