# Voice of the Pro

Quotes and paraphrased sentiment from Pros (contractors/home-service owners) on
getting paid, from public reviews and trade forums. Gathered 2026-07-06.

## On non-payment and chasing money

- *"Has anyone else noticed lately how many customers are not paying their bills
  or invoices? Maybe it's the economy but I have so many not paying their invoices
  for our work at all."* — HVAC (heating, ventilation & air conditioning)
  contractor, [HVAC Site forum](https://www.hvacsite.com/threads/anyone-dealing-with-some-non-paying-customers.87/)
- On legal recourse for bounced checks/non-payment: *"the problem is in most cases
  the unpaid bill is cheaper than paying the lawyer."* — same thread. Small
  balances effectively have no enforcement mechanism; the reminder/relationship
  channel is all a Pro has.
- Contractors who avoid the problem largely do it with **process discipline**:
  payment due on completion, deposits on large jobs, walking the customer through
  the invoice on site. ([ACCA (Air Conditioning Contractors of America) blog on non-paying customers](https://hvac-blog.acca.org/what-do-i-do-when-a-customer-doesnt-pay/))

## On Housecall Pro's reminder/invoicing experience

- *"The invoicing is very confusing and has glitches, which is a major problem when
  a customer gets 5 late payment requests after they've paid."* — Housecall Pro (HCP) reviewer
  ([Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/housecallpro.com?page=7)). A
  wrong reminder is worse than no reminder: it embarrasses the Pro with their
  customer.
- Reviewers report being **unable to edit and save default email text**, forcing
  copy-paste of custom messaging into every invoice sent; reminder settings are
  described as difficult to configure. ([Capterra reviews](https://www.capterra.com/p/140363/HouseCall-Pro/reviews/))
- Complaints that long-requested billing features (e.g., automatic late fees) sit
  unresolved while other features ship. ([Capterra reviews](https://www.capterra.com/p/140363/HouseCall-Pro/reviews/))

## Recurring themes

1. **The relationship constraint.** Pros live in small communities and depend on
   repeat business and referrals. They want to get paid *without* sounding like a
   collections agency. Tone matters as much as timing.
2. **Embarrassment risk cuts both ways.** Dunning (repeatedly demanding payment on
   an overdue bill) a customer who already paid (or
   who has a legitimate dispute) damages trust faster than the unpaid invoice did.
3. **No back office.** The person chasing invoices is usually the owner, at night,
   after a full day in the field. Anything that requires "managing a system"
   (ServiceTitan-style AR workflows) doesn't fit a 1–10-person shop.
4. **Enforcement is hollow below ~$2–5K.** Liens and lawyers don't pencil out, so
   persistence and communication quality are the only real levers.
